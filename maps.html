EPIC MAPS

Internal Tool For IT Team

On the Verona, WI campus, Epic employs over 10,000 people and in over 30 different buildings. As a member of the Computer and Techonology Services department, I spend a good portion of my time traversing the campus and making deliveries of computer equipment for employees. With each building having 150+ offices, navigating can be extremely time consuming.


Epic’s internal website currently has a map system, but it is very limited as you can only look up one office at a time. My team does deliveries for upwards of 20 people per run, so it was a huge hassle to search for each office individually.


I fully developed an internal tool that allows for input of any number of offices, and will ouput a webpage with every room in every building highlighted.

Features and Improvements


When I first created this tool, it started off as very barebones. It would only work by taking input from a seperate internal tool webpage, and would display a hardly readable list of map images. Here is a timeline of the features and improvements I added.


Zoom

With different employees working on different sized screens, with different workflows (full-screen view vs. split-screen), I added a slider bar to adjust the zoom level of the maps. This way, you could easily zoom in closer on a map, or view multiple maps at a time.


Labels

Some of the maps were hard to read, and it was hard to tell the building and floor level of a map image. I added map labels to each map, and I found the geometric center of each room to apply room number labels.


Custom Input

Most deliveries were done based off a seperate internal tool, but a lot were done based off of seperate office lists. I added a feature to allow for custom input based on a user-entered list of offices. It also accounted for errors such as misformatting and building abbrevations.


Check-Off

When delivering to mulitple offices on a floor, it can be hard to keep track of which rooms you have already been to. I added a check-off feature, such that when you click on an office on the map, it will become greyed out. Once all offices on a map image are checked, the entire image will become greyed out


Printer-Friendly Maps

Some of my colleagues prefer to leave their laptop behind during deliveries, as it takes up too much space. They would try to print the page, but it became very difficult to read on paper. I added a “Print Maps” button to reformat the webpage in a printable format.


Hover

With the interactivity of Maps and the other internal tool, there was a lot of clicking back and forth between tabs. To reduce this, when a user hovered over an office name on the other tool, a thumbnail pop-up image of that room highlighted on a map would appear.


Next Steps - Maps 2.0


This tool has made it drastically easier for my colleagues to make deliveries. It also serves as a great visualization tool to quickly see what buildings have yet to be delivered to. That being said, there are still plenty of improvements that can be made.


I am currently in the process of revamping the logic of the entire component in order to reduce load times and account for future updates. I am drastically improving the appearance and readability of each map image, as I am adding labels to every room, rather than just the highlighted ones. I’ll also be using more color and interactivity to each room, to make it more in line with the appearance and functionality of Epic’s current map system.
